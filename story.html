Free diving — without tanks, without noise — became a practice in presence. The discipline of breath-hold training fed directly into how I approach hard problems: slow down, conserve energy, go deeper than is comfortable, and trust yourself to surface. The FON water sporting community in Cochin was my tribe for four years, and those early mornings on the Arabian Sea are some of the most honest hours of my life.

02

Discipline · Combat · Mind

Mixed martial arts
and the art of falling.

Mixed martial arts taught me something that no engineering textbook could: how to be comfortable being uncomfortable. You can't think your way out of an armbar. You have to train your body until the right response is instinct.

The mat is a great equaliser. Rank, degree, title — none of it matters when you're sparring. What matters is preparation, humility, and the willingness to get back up. I carry those three things into every technical challenge.

04

Creativity · Imagination · Story

The worlds
inside stories.

Anime isn't just entertainment. The best of it — Fullmetal Alchemist, Vinland Saga, Steins;Gate — are meditations on sacrifice, time, and what it means to chase something impossible with everything you have.

I grew up watching stories of engineers and scientists who refused to accept the limits of what was known. That's not incidental to my career — it's foundational to it. Good science fiction taught me to think in systems before I knew what systems thinking was.

The same imagination that keeps me watching anime at midnight is the one that asks: what if we could tokenise renewable energy on a blockchain? What if second-life EV batteries could stabilise the grid? What if a 17-year-old in Kerala could build a brain-controlled exoskeleton?

Giving back was never an obligation — it was a compulsion. At Volship India I joined other IT professionals to teach underprivileged students the fundamentals of computers. Some of those kids had never touched a laptop. Watching someone understand how a for-loop works for the first time is one of the most satisfying things I've experienced. It reminded me why I got into this field.

07

Vision · Future · Now

What drives
the machine.

I didn't get into sustainable energy because it's fashionable. I got into it because as a teenager in Kerala I watched monsoon patterns shift, because I sat in a TATA Power plant during an internship and felt the weight of what it means to keep millions of people connected to electricity.

The brain-controlled exoskeleton wasn't just a school project. It was a question: what if the boundary between human intention and physical action could dissolve? That question evolved — today it's about the boundary between energy generation and energy authentication, between renewable electrons and trusted data.

Every dive, every fight on the mat, every anime episode finished at 2am, every line of code written in a second language in a country that isn't mine — it's all part of the same project. To understand the world deeply enough to change some small part of it.
